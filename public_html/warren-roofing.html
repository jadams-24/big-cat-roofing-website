Expert Roofing Solutions for Michigan's Weather Challenges

As a local roofing company, we build and repair roofs specifically designed to withstand Michigan's demanding climate. We protect your home or business from:

Heavy Snow & Structural Stress

Michigan's heavy snowfall can strain your roof's structure. Our new roof installations are engineered to exceed local snow load requirements. We also offer roof inspections to assess your current roof's integrity and perform preventative maintenance before winter hits.

Ice Dam Prevention & Leaking Roofs

The constant freeze-thaw cycle in Macomb County is the primary cause of ice dams, which can lead to water backing up under your shingles and causing costly interior damage. Our roof repair services focus on a total system approach, including installing proper attic ventilation and a complete ice & water shield to eliminate ice dams for good.

High Winds & Storm Damage Repair

Severe thunderstorms can cause significant wind damage and lift shingles, exposing your home. We are emergency roof repair specialists, responding quickly after a storm. We install high-wind-rated materials like GAF Timberline HDZ® shingles, which are designed to protect your home from the most severe weather events.

Extreme Temperature Durability

Summer heat and winter cold cause constant expansion and contraction, leading to cracked shingles and sealant failure. We use premium materials for both residential roof replacement and commercial flat roofs (TPO & EPDM) that are proven to endure Michigan's temperature extremes and provide decades of protection.

Frequently Asked Questions - Local Roofing

Do I need a permit for roof repair in Warren, MI?

Yes, the city requires permits for most roofing work, including full replacements and major repairs. Minor repairs may not require permits. Big Cat Roofing handles all permit applications and ensures compliance with local building codes. Contact the Building Department at (586) 574-4680 for specific requirements.

How does the inland location affect my roof compared to lakefront cities?

The inland location means less lake-effect weather but more exposure to severe thunderstorms, tornadoes, and temperature fluctuations. The city experiences typical Midwest weather patterns including heavy snow loads, ice dams, and summer hail storms. Regular maintenance helps protect against these inland weather challenges.

What's the average cost of roofing locally?

Roof replacement typically ranges from $11,000 to $19,000 for average-sized homes, depending on materials and complexity. The area's mix of mid-century and newer construction affects pricing. We provide free, detailed estimates based on your specific property and needs.
